Background

Nepal faced long-term political instability, corruption, and limited economic opportunities for youth. Generation Z, born between the late 1990s and early 2010s, became frustrated with the elites. Youth unemployment and government scandals fueled anger. The 2025 social media ban triggered protests.

Causes

Corruption & Nepotism

Viral videos exposing politicians' lavish lifestyles increased public anger. [Reddit]

Economic Frustration

High youth unemployment and limited opportunities fueled the protests. [Britannica]

Social & Political Inequality

Long-standing social disparities and youth marginalization contributed to protests.

Timeline

8 September 2025

Protests began in Kathmandu and other cities. Police responded with tear gas and live ammunition. [HRW]

9 September 2025

Parliament was stormed and set on fire. PM K.P. Sharma Oli resigned. [Wikipedia]

10тАУ13 September 2025

Curfew imposed. Sushila Karki appointed interim PM. Protests gradually decreased. [Wikipedia]

Victims

76 people died, including protesters, police, and civilians. Over 2,000 were injured. Many victims were youth participating peacefully. [Wikipedia]

Security forces were deployed, curfews imposed, and social media blocked to prevent protest organization online.
